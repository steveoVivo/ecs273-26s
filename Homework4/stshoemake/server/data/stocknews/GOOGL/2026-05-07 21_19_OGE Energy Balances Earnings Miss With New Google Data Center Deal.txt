OGE Energy (NYSE:OGE) reported first quarter earnings that fell short of expectations, with results affected by mild weather and higher maintenance expenses.
The company also announced a power supply agreement with Google to serve three new data centers, a deal that will require regulatory approval.
The combination of weaker quarterly earnings and the new data center contract highlights a shift in OGE Energy's near term performance and long term commitments.
OGE Energy enters this news cycle with its stock trading at $47.73 and a return of 11.3% year to date. Over the past 5 years, the stock has returned 80.0%, and 42.9% over the past 3 years, placing current developments against a backdrop of meaningful multi year gains.
For investors, the Google agreement introduces a new, high profile customer relationship that could influence how OGE Energy allocates capital and plans future capacity, subject to regulatory approval. At the same time, the recent earnings miss highlights operational and weather related sensitivities that may remain in focus as new demand from data centers comes into play.
