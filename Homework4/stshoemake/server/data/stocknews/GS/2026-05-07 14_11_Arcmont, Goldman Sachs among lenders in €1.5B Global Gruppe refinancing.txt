A large club of direct lenders is providing roughly €1.5 billion in loans to refinance German insurance brokerage Global Gruppe, in one of the largest European direct lending deals this year, according to market sources.
Lenders including Goldman Sachs, Arcmont, HPS, SMBC, KKR Credit, and Morgan Stanley are among those providing the financing, the sources said.
The refinancing comes after the group — which is backed by Castik Capital Partners and Summit Partners — withdrew a sale process due to a valuation mismatch. LCD reported the group was marketed at EBITDA of roughly €170 million, was exploring a refinancing, and that existing lenders Goldman Sachs and HPS had been keen to support the refinancing.
Houlihan Lokey, which advised Global Gruppe on the refinancing, said in an online post that the deal closed in April.
Castik Capital, Arcmont, HPS, and Morgan Stanley did not respond to requests for comment. KKR Credit, SMBC, Goldman Sachs, and Houlihan Lokey declined to comment.
Castik acquired a stake in Global Gruppe in 2023. Based in Cologne, the company provides insurance and risk management services to medium-sized companies in manufacturing, retail, and real estate. It employs more than 1,500 people across Germany, Austria, Switzerland and the Netherlands.
