Exxon Mobil (NYSE:XOM) is starting to move faster on oil exploration, and AI is playing a big role in that shift, helping the company turn what used to take months into something that now takes just days.
The idea is fairly simple but powerful. Exxon is using machine learning to go through massive amounts of seismic data and quickly flag the most promising drilling spots. That means geologists can spend less time sorting through raw data and more time focusing on high value opportunities. The approach is already being used in Guyana, where Exxon is part of a consortium producing more than 900K barrels per day, and it is now being applied to other regions as well.

What makes this more interesting is how it changes the economics. With better tools, Exxon is revisiting older discoveries that were once considered too expensive to develop, but could now make sense. The company is also planning new surveys in places like Trinidad and Tobago, with the expectation that faster analysis will lead to quicker decisions. At the same time, it is sticking with its core low cost assets, including Guyana and the Permian Basin, where break even costs are below $30 per barrel.
