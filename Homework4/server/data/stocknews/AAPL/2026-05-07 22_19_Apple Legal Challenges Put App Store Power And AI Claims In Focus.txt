Rave Inc. has filed antitrust lawsuits against Apple in multiple countries, alleging anti-competitive removal of its app from the App Store.
Apple, ticker NasdaqGS:AAPL, has agreed to a $250 million class-action settlement related to claims of misleading advertising of AI features in Siri.
The lawsuits and settlement raise questions about Apple's App Store policies, platform power and transparency in marketing its AI capabilities.
Apple runs a large hardware and services ecosystem centered on the iPhone, Mac, wearables and the App Store, with AI features increasingly woven into its products. In this context, the disputes with Rave Inc. and the Siri-related settlement come as regulators, developers and consumers pay closer attention to how big platforms treat apps and present AI features.
For you as an investor, these cases matter because they relate to regulatory exposure, potential changes to App Store rules and how customers view the Apple brand. Outcomes are uncertain, and the direction of these issues could influence how Apple manages its platform, shares AI product information and balances control with third party developers over time.
